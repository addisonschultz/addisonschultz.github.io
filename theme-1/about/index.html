Well, I’m 23 years old, and constantly learning new things. I grew up in a small town in Wisconsin, USA, and moved to Milwaukee when I was 18 to study Digital Marketing and Information Technology at The University of Wisconsin Milwaukee.

When I was in University, I realised how important computers were becoming in the world, and found it interesting learning how everything worked behind the screen. So, I started teaching myself anything and everything, and found it so satisfying to see my work have a part in contributing to the web.

Throughout studying, I was always seeing how I could apply the things I was learning into real-life projects that would benefit others and the way they interacted with the internet.

The day after I graduated from University, I was on a plane to Amsterdam. I had a bag of clothes, a short-term place to stay, and an empty mind that was willing to learn.

While I was in Amsterdam, I was still able to freelance, while continuing learning about modern Full Stack Web Development in more practical settings at a school called Codaisseur.

This eventually led me to where I am now, where I get to work with and advise companies around the globe, learning about how product teams work, but also helping them understand some of the fundamentals I’ve learned with Framer.
